Dirty Thunderstorms



A Strange Natural Phenomena

What on earth is a dirty thunderstorm? And why have you not heard of such a thing? If you do a google image search on 'dirty thunderstorms', with the first image you see, you'll tell yourself that this is a gateway to hell. Then proceed to turn off the computer and try to tell yourself that it's a still from a movie. On the ☼ side, it is not a movie nor a supernatural event. Asscosiated with volcanic eruptions, dirty thunderstorms are a rare weather phenomenon where lightning forms inside a volcanic plume (mixture of volcanic gas and particles).

Large volcanic eruptions? How does that happen?
Mainly, lightening forms when ice crystals collide and generate electric charges. Except in a volcanic eruption, cloud ash particles (i.e. rock fragments and ice particles) collide instead of ice crystals. Despite that lightning usually descends from storm clouds in the sky, dirty thunderstorms are generated within the ash cloud spewing from the volcano. However, this amazing and terrifying occurance is not fully understand by scientists as there is little research done because of how dangerously it effects the environment nearby, including people.

Did you know?

  • Dirty thunderstorms occur regularly at the Sakurajima volcano in Japan, one of the most active volcanoes in the world.

  • The most recently recorded occurance was 7/26/16 at Mount Sakurajima.

  • Calbuco, one of the most dangerous volcano in Chile, erupted in April 2015 for the first time in 42 years. It covered the surrounding areas in thick ash, forced thousands to evacuate. However, the dirty thunderstorm was caught on camera. Click to watch
